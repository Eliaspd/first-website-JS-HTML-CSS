Excelsa

Excelsa coffee beans, also known as Coffea excelsa. Excelsa, are one of the lesser-known coffee species compared to Arabica and Robusta but still play a significant role in the world of coffee.

While Excelsa coffee is not as widely cultivated or traded as Arabica and Robusta, it remains an important cash crop in certain regions of West and Central Africa, contributing to the local economy. They are primarily used in coffee blends to add complexity and acidity, and they are more commonly found in regions where Liberica coffee is cultivated. While less well-known in the global coffee market, Excelsa coffee has its own unique appeal and characteristics.
