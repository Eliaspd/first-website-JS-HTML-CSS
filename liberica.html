Liberica

Liberica coffee beans, scientifically known as Coffea liberica, are one of the four primary species of coffee beans, alongside Arabica (Coffea arabica), Robusta (Coffea canephora), and Excelsa (Coffea excelsa). While less common and less well-known than Arabica and Robusta, Liberica coffee beans have their own unique characteristics and are cultivated in specific regions.

Liberica coffee is known for its unique and distinct flavor profile. It often has a woody, smoky, and earthy taste with a full body. In summary, Liberica coffee beans are known for their unique and distinct flavor, larger plant size, and suitability for challenging growing conditions. While they are less commonly found in global coffee markets compared to Arabica and Robusta, they remain an important crop in specific regions, and some efforts are underway to promote their cultivation and consumption.
