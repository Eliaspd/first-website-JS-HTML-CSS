Robusta

Robusta coffee beans, scientifically known as Coffea canephora, are one of the two primary species of coffee beans, the other being Arabica (Coffea arabica). Robusta beans have their own distinct characteristics, origins, and uses in the coffee industry.

Robusta coffee is known for its strong, bitter taste. It often has a more harsh flavor compared to the smoother and milder Arabica coffee. The coffee is often described as having nutty and woody flavor notes, with hints of earthiness. Robusta coffee is prized for its ability to produce a thick, creamy layer of crema on espresso shots, making it a popular choice for espresso blends. In summary, Robusta coffee beans are known for their strong, bitter flavor, higher caffeine content, and robust growth characteristics. They are a crucial component of the global coffee industry, offering unique flavor profiles and economic opportunities for coffee-producing regions.
